Fortochka

Posted on January 1, 2012 by jo

In Russian mythology, Fortochka is an unwelcome spectre of narrative dissatisfaction.

In Â many versions of “Gregor and the Dragon”, for example, Fortochka convinces Gregor to take up potato farming in Uzbekistan rather than face the dragon. In “Mischa’s deal with the Devil”, Fortochka is sometimes seen taking Mischa to a legal advisor who helps him with his infernal contract.

Some researchers argue that Fortochka is more likely to make an appearance late at night, when babushkas get sleepy and the story needs a quick ending. Others, however, claim that Fortochka is the foremost, unsung, villain of Russian fairy tale.

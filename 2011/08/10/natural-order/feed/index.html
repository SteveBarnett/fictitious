Wed, 12 Sep 2012 11:01:06 +0000 hourly 1 http://wordpress.org/?v=3.8.10 By: jo http://fictitious.co.za/2011/08/10/natural-order/#comment-44 Tue, 23 Aug 2011 08:28:20 +0000 http://fictitious.co.za/?p=122#comment-44 I loved the idea of this – the scary thought of the world just breaking up, losing its flavour (it reminded me of Stephen King’s Langolliers in this respect – but that is not an end of the world story.

Agree about the tense – switching – I think past tense throughout with just the last paragraph in present tense would be better (the whole thing in present tense would be weird).

Line by line nitpicks (as I’m feeling frisky):
“We all saw The Event happen.” – small T?
“The half of the world that was asleep woke up, and felt compelled to step outside and tilt our heads back.” – slightly confused ownership of heads: “The half of the world that was asleep woke up. We all felt compelled to step outside and tilt our heads back”?
“…standing in the garden in our pajamas”, – again the our is confusing (humanity? you and your family?) I would go with “standing in our gardens in pajamas”
“aftereffects” – after-effects?
“Did we check us?” – this is a little hard to parse
“It was the little things … if they worked at all.” – this whole paragraph is great, gives a real flavour to the dulling of the world.

” Years had become longer.” – this is also cool. I like the idea of everyone getting tired as days and hours get longer, but no one notices as clocks slow.
““Everything is going to shit,” I think was the official line from the President’s office.” – this doesn’t work for me, I can’t see that announcement happening so it breaks the flow for me.

Great story, depressing and disturbing.

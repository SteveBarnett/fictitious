All these hints and outright glimpses of wrongness hiding everywhere. I very much liked the unusual ways the place was… well, unusual – the smell of the sea, the sticky tables, the strange mantelpiece. Delightful and unexpected! The language leaps off the page – “the smell of meat before it turns”, “listed towards each other like drunkards”, “cleaned up in a hurry upon hearing the doorbell”. Unusual and evocative. The atmosphere just teams with soupy horror.

Things that did not quite work for me – the haberdashery, the occult symbols, perhaps the claw marks too. A little more obvious than the more unexpected other signs. But this is a minor quib. Great story!
